04
Market & PMF
Where positioning strength aligns with customer fit — and where it must evolve.
Fit vs. Friction

The moat moved.

Where fit is strongest

The operator already owns a trusted, recurring client relationship — and watches those clients drive across town for GLP-1s.

Asher converts that leakage into high-margin recurring revenue in 14–48 hours, with zero clinical build.

Where it must evolve

The early pitch leaned on the price arbitrage of cheap compounded semaglutide. That moat is eroding from two directions.

Regulatory: 503B mass-compounding is closing. Price: branded DTC has collapsed toward the compounded range.

The durable pitch is compliance + done-for-you infrastructure + the client relationship — not the lowest semaglutide price.
A positioning lever the Sales Director owns — not a product problem to wait on.
